Pointara — Privacy Policy

Effective date: 10 July 2026
App: Pointara (Android)
Developer: Oakstone Software
Contact: dan@oakstonesoftware.co.uk

The short version

Pointara has no user accounts and does not sell your data or share it with data brokers. We do not operate any servers of our own, so we never store your personal data anywhere. The app shows banner ads through Google AdMob to help cover the cost of running it for free — this is the only advertising or third-party SDK in the app, and it's described fully in the Advertising section below, including how to control it. Your location is used to find places near you, and the only time it leaves your device is as part of the map and place-search requests sent to the OpenStreetMap services described below; your precise location is never shared with the ad network.

Information the app uses

Location (precise or approximate)

Pointara is a compass that points you toward nearby food, drink, amenities and places of interest. To do that, it uses your device's location, with your permission, for the following purposes only:

  • calculating the distance and direction from you to nearby places, on your device;
  • requesting a list of nearby places from OpenStreetMap's Overpass API, which requires sending the coordinates of your search area;
  • requesting map images ("tiles") for the area around you when the map overlay is turned on.

Your location is processed in real time for each request and is not stored by us. You can use the app with location permission denied by choosing a simulated preset location, and you can withdraw location permission at any time in Android settings.

Data stored on your device only

The app saves the following on your device and nowhere else: your favourites, selected categories and search range, theme and map preferences, and a temporary cache of recently fetched nearby places (kept so the app works offline and to reduce network use). This data never leaves your device, and you can erase all of it at any time via Android Settings → Apps → Pointara → Clear data, or by uninstalling the app.

If you have Android's device backup enabled, Android may include the app's settings in your encrypted Google device backup. That backup is controlled by you and Google, not by us.

Notifications

If you turn on nearby-place notifications, they are generated entirely on your device. No notification data is sent anywhere.

What we do NOT collect

We do not collect or have access to: your name, email address, contacts, photos, files, microphone, or camera. We do not run our own analytics or crash-reporting SDKs of any kind. The only third-party SDK in the app is Google AdMob, used solely to show ads — see the Advertising section below for exactly what that involves.

Advertising

Pointara shows banner ads, served through Google AdMob, to help cover the cost of running the app for free. This is the only commercial or ad-related data use in the app.

To choose and serve ads, Google AdMob (and the ad networks it works with) may collect and process: your advertising ID (a resettable, non-permanent device identifier — Pointara has no accounts, so this is never linked to your name or identity), general device and app information (device model, OS version, app version), an approximate location derived from your IP address, and ad interaction data such as impressions and clicks. This is separate from, and does not include, the precise or simulated location you grant Pointara for compass functionality — that location is never passed to AdMob or any ad network.

Where UK/EU data protection and e-privacy law applies (which covers accessing an advertising identifier on your device), Pointara shows a consent form — via Google's User Messaging Platform — the first time you open the app, letting you choose whether to allow personalised advertising, limit ads to non-personalised only, or manage that choice later. You can review or withdraw consent at any time from the app's privacy settings, and you can reset or limit ad tracking for your whole device at any time via Android Settings → Google → Ads. Choosing non-personalised ads does not stop ads from working — you'll still see ads, just not ones based on your interests.

Ads are not targeted at children, and consistent with Google's ad-serving protections for minors, Pointara does not knowingly serve personalised ads to any user it identifies as under 18.

For more detail on how Google itself handles this data, see Google's Privacy Policy and How Google uses information from sites or apps that use our services.

Services that receive requests from the app

To show places and maps, the app sends requests directly from your device to the following publicly operated OpenStreetMap services. Like any web server, they receive your IP address alongside each request, and the requests include the coordinates of the map area being viewed or searched:

  • Overpass API instances (place search): overpass-api.de (Germany), overpass.kumi.systems (Austria), overpass.osm.ch (Switzerland), overpass.nchc.org.tw (Taiwan)
  • OpenStreetMap tile servers (standard map images): tile.openstreetmap.org, operated by the OpenStreetMap Foundation (UK)
  • CyclOSM tile servers (cycle map images): tile-cyclosm.openstreetmap.fr, operated by OpenStreetMap France
  • Google AdMob (advertising): serves the ads shown in the app and may receive your advertising ID, device information, and an IP-derived approximate location, as described in the Advertising section above.

These services are independent of us and process requests under their own policies — see the OpenStreetMap Foundation Privacy Policy and Google's Privacy Policy. Aside from AdMob as described above, we send the other services only what is necessary to answer each request, and we receive no information about you from them. All connections use HTTPS encryption.

Map data is © OpenStreetMap contributors, available under the Open Database Licence.

Legal basis (UK GDPR)

Where UK/EU data protection law applies, our basis for processing your location is your consent, given when you grant the location permission, and our legitimate interest in answering the map and place requests you initiate. You may withdraw consent at any time by revoking the permission in Android settings; the app remains usable with a simulated location.

For advertising identifiers and ad personalisation, our basis is your consent, gathered through the in-app consent form on first use and adjustable at any time as described in the Advertising section. If you don't consent to personalised ads, Pointara shows non-personalised, contextual ads instead.

Data retention

We retain nothing ourselves: we have no servers and no copies of your data. Data on your device (favourites, settings, place cache) remains until you clear it or uninstall the app. Advertising data such as your advertising ID and ad interaction history is processed and retained by Google/AdMob under their own retention policies, not ours — see Google's Privacy Policy for details.

Your rights

Under UK GDPR you have rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability and objection. Because we hold no personal data about you ourselves, these rights are in practice exercised through your own device (clearing app data, revoking permissions, resetting your advertising ID, or withdrawing ad consent as described in the Advertising section). For data processed by Google as part of ad serving, you can also exercise your rights directly with Google — see their Privacy Policy for how. If you have any question or concern, contact us at the email above; you also have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ico.org.uk).

Children

Pointara is not directed at children under 13. We collect no personal data from any user ourselves, and ads are not personalised for anyone the app identifies as under 18, as described in the Advertising section. Younger users should play with a parent or guardian's involvement, particularly because the app encourages real-world walking.

Security

All network traffic uses HTTPS. All personal preferences remain on your device under Android's standard app sandboxing.

Changes to this policy

If we add features that change how data is handled (for example optional analytics or accounts), we will update this policy, change the effective date above, and describe the change in the app's update notes before it takes effect.
